Variational AutoEncoder (VAE)

The VAE is a neural network architecture that works by âencodingâ data (such as a midi file) in such a way that the data can be represented in a compressed form that describes where that data point exists in the total possible âdata spaceâ. This data space is referred to as latent space in VAEs and can be described by parameters such as the mean and standard deviation, similar to a Gaussian distribution. By constructing this latent space and randomly sampling it, it is possible to get an output that is similar to other data points in the training data, but not exactly like any other data point. This is what makes VAEs exciting for creative generative modeling.

Generative Adversarial Network (GAN)

Generative adversarial networks (GANs) are algorithmic architectures that use two neural networks, pitting one against the other (thus the âadversarialâ) in order to generate new, synthetic instances of data that can pass for real data. They are used widely in image generation, video generation, and voice generation, but can also be applied in such cases to generate music.
